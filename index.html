The Canadian Independent Fish Harvesters Federation (CIFHF) brings together fishing associations from British Columbia to Newfoundland, representing the collective voice of independent harvesters who depend on sustainable fisheries for their families and communities.

Governance

How the Federation Is Governed

The Canadian Independent Fish Harvesters Federation operates through a democratic governance structure that ensures every member organization has a voice.

Constitutional Convention

The supreme governing body of the Federation. All member organizations send delegates to set policy direction and amend the constitution.

Board of Directors

Elected representatives from each member organization who oversee operations, approve budgets, and guide strategic direction between conventions.

Executive Committee

The President and executive members who manage day-to-day operations, represent the Federation publicly, and execute Board decisions.

What We Stand For

Objectives of the
Canadian Independent Fish Harvesters Federation

Protecting independent harvesters, preserving coastal communities, and ensuring sustainable fisheries for future generations.

Core Objectives

Shared Vision, Unified Action

Primary Mission

To protect and advance the interests of independent, owner-operator fish harvesters across Canada through coordinated national advocacy, policy development, and representation before federal government bodies.

Shared Vision

A Canadian fishing industry where independent harvesters control their licences and enterprises, coastal communities thrive, and marine resources are managed sustainably for the benefit of current and future generations.

Core Activities

  • National lobbying and government relations
  • Parliamentary committee testimony and policy briefs
  • Inter-provincial coordination on shared fisheries issues
  • Public awareness campaigns on harvester rights
  • Research and data analysis on fisheries economics

Accountability

Tracking government commitments, grading federal responses to parliamentary recommendations, and holding decision-makers accountable for the promises they make to fishing communities.

Key Policy Positions

The Canadian Independent Fish Harvesters Federation advocates on critical policy fronts that protect independent harvesters and the communities they sustain.

Atlantic Canada

  • Fleet Separation Policy — keeping fishing licences and processing separate
  • Owner-Operator Policy — licence holders must be on the vessel
  • PIIFCAF — Preserving the Independence of the Inshore Fleet in Canada's Atlantic Fisheries
  • Opposition to corporate consolidation of fishing rights

British Columbia

  • Advocacy for owner-operator protections on the Pacific coast
  • Opposition to licence leasing and armchair fishing
  • Fair access to quota and licensing for working harvesters
  • Support for Pacific salmon conservation and rebuilding

Why Accountability Matters

When Parliament's Standing Committee on Fisheries and Oceans (FOPO) issues recommendations, they represent the voices of harvesters, scientists, and coastal communities who testified under oath. But recommendations without follow-through are just words on paper.

Our Accountability Report grades the federal government's response to 51 recommendations covering owner-operator protections, IUU fishing enforcement, and fisheries rebuilding. The findings are clear: progress has been made on some fronts, but critical gaps remain—particularly in enforcement against illegal harvesting and rebuilding depleted fish stocks.

With $789.6 million spent on NIHB medical transportation in 2023–24 and only a fraction directed at fisheries enforcement, the allocation of federal resources demands scrutiny. Independent harvesters deserve a government that matches its commitments with action.
